\section{Technical summary}

This report documents the staged placement of 1,726 global L4 risk cards into a
new hierarchy containing 50 L3 nodes. The 182 classifications inherited from
the Physical AI taxonomy are treated as a locked gold set. The legacy global
L1--L3 hierarchy is retained only as reference metadata and is not used as a
prediction feature. Permanent L4 identifiers and source crosswalks are
preserved throughout the process.

Stage 1 applies strict rule--semantic--anchor agreement and requires two expert
agents to agree on the exact L3. It retains the 182 Physical locks and proposes
only 37 additional placements. Stage 2 relaxes the open-set threshold and
places 1,553 cards (89.9768\%), leaving 173 for taxonomy review. Stage 3 tests
100\% operational coverage by forcing all 173 remaining cards to their recorded
hierarchy-blind top-1 L3. The final policy view then removes the 55 hard holds
from the taxonomy and retains 1,671 placements plus 55 review holds.

\section{Scope, data, and identification rules}

The unit of analysis is one L4 card with a permanent RAI4 identifier. The
identifier range runs from 0001 through 1726. Labels are not used as identifiers
because identical names may occur on different paths. The hierarchy contains
one L0 root, three L1 domains, six L2 groups, and 50 L3 nodes. \code{RAI-HOLD}
is an operational review bucket and is not an L3 node.

\section{Staged classification process and decision criteria}

\subsection{Stage 0: canonicalization and protected sets}

Before classification, the global and Physical sources are joined through the
source crosswalk and assigned permanent RAI4 IDs. The Physical AI site's 182
existing L4-to-L3 classifications form a protected gold set. Their paths cannot
be changed by later algorithmic stages. The remaining 1,544 cards enter the
hierarchy-blind classification pipeline.

\subsection{Stage 1: strict open-set classification}

For each non-Physical card, the model input contains only the L4 label, a direct
mechanism definition with hierarchy restatements removed, and the evidence
title. Legacy global L0--L3 values, source family, and source-ID prefixes are
excluded from prediction features. A Stage 1 automatic candidate must satisfy
all of the following criteria:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item Exactly one L3 is rule-eligible.
  \item The rule winner, semantic top-1, and composite top-1 are the same L3.
  \item Top-1 semantic score is at least 0.55 and composite score is at least 0.54.
  \item Both semantic and composite margins are at least 0.035.
  \item At least two of three anchor views select the same L3 as top-1.
  \item No taxonomy-gap sentinel, multi-mechanism flag, or Physical-boundary flag is present.
  \item Two independent expert agents approve the same exact L3.
\end{enumerate}

This gate yields 37 new expert-consensus proposals. Together with the 182
Physical locks, Stage 1 assigns 219 cards and withholds 1,507.

\subsection{Stage 2: relaxed classification targeting 90\% coverage}

Stage 2 preserves all 219 protected placements and ranks the Stage 1 holds with
the deterministic suitability score
\[
S_2=0.55q_{sem}+0.20q_{sm}+0.10q_{cm}+0.10q_{vote}+0.05q_{rule}-p_{gap}.
\]
The normalized terms are defined as
\begin{align*}
q_{sem}&=\operatorname{clip}((s_{sem}-0.45)/0.25,0,1),\\
q_{sm}&=\operatorname{clip}(m_{sem}/0.05,0,1),\\
q_{cm}&=\operatorname{clip}(m_{comp}/0.05,0,1),\\
q_{vote}&=v_{anchor}/3,\\
q_{rule}&=\mathbf{1}[\text{at least one eligible L3}],\\
p_{gap}&=0.05\,\mathbf{1}[\text{gap sentinel present}].
\end{align*}

The score is a review-ordering measure, not a calibrated probability. Stage 2
first preserves 55 hard holds regardless of score. It then sorts all remaining
holds by ascending $S_2$, using permanent RAI4 ID as the deterministic tie
breaker, and reserves the bottom 118. The final Stage 2 hold set is therefore
$55+118=173$ cards (10.0232\%). All other cards are assigned to their recorded
hierarchy-blind top-1 L3, producing 1,553 placements (89.9768\%).

\subsection{Stage 3: forced matching test}

Stage 3 does not retrain or rescore the model. It preserves the 1,553 non-null
Stage 2 placements and assigns only the 173 Stage 2 holds to the top-1 L3 already
recorded in the algorithm score artifact. Thresholds, margins, rule eligibility,
and gap sentinels no longer block assignment. Every new placement is marked
\code{forced\_match\_stage3}; the original hold reason, hard/quota class, score,
review priority, and \code{human\_approved=false} flag remain visible. This
experiment achieves 1,726/1,726 operational paths but does not establish that
the 50 L3 taxonomy is sufficient.

\subsection{Final policy: separate the 55 hard holds}

The final policy view reverses the forced taxonomy placement for five hard-hold
families. Their policy L3 is set to null and their operational bucket is set to
\code{RAI-HOLD}. The 118 quota holds remain forced candidates requiring human
review. The forced top-1 L3 for each of the 55 holds remains available only as
audit metadata.

The hold bucket must not be presented as an unapproved 51st L3. The website and
exports should show it as a separate review queue and retain the original hold
reason, forced-candidate L3, semantic and composite scores, anchor votes, and
critical review priority. The 24 expert rejection/disagreement cases test L3
definition fit; the six multi-mechanism cases test whether primary/secondary or
multi-label structure is needed; and the 23 Physical-boundary cases require
comparison against the locked Physical taxonomy.

\section{Validation, limitations, and robustness checks}

The 182 Physical placements and 37 Stage 1 expert-consensus placements remain
unchanged in all later stages. The Stage 2 and Stage 3 builders reproduce
byte-identical placement hashes on repeated runs. The Stage 2 validator reports
13 PASS, 0 FAIL, and 3 WARN; the Stage 3 validator reports 14 PASS, 0 FAIL, and
4 WARN. All 16 repository regression tests, including the final hold-policy
tests, pass.

Material limitations are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item 1,214 of the 1,334 new Stage 2 placements lack Stage 1 rule eligibility and rely primarily on semantic top-1 proximity.
  \item 247 new Stage 2 placements override a taxonomy-gap sentinel.
  \item The remaining 118 Stage 3 forced candidates are neither calibrated probabilities nor human approvals.
  \item The Physical-boundary sentinel uses a limited keyword set and can produce both false positives and false negatives.
  \item Use of all 50 L3 nodes and 96.8134\% card coverage do not prove taxonomy sufficiency.
\end{itemize}

\section{Recommended review sequence and open questions}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item Review all 55 \code{RAI-HOLD} cards, prioritizing the 23 Physical-boundary cases and 24 expert rejection/disagreement cases.
  \item Review the 118 Stage 3 quota candidates in ascending $S_2$ order.
  \item Audit stratified L3 samples from the 247 gap overrides and 1,214 rule-free placements.
  \item Use the evidence to select a remedy: expand an L3, add an L3, or permit primary and secondary labels.
  \item Publish only human-approved migrations. Preserve decision IDs and history in the successor release.
\end{enumerate}

Open decisions include how many Physical-boundary cards belong in the Physical
taxonomy, whether multi-mechanism cards may receive a secondary L3, what human
review error tolerance is acceptable for the 118 forced candidates, and what
minimum evidence should be required for proposing a new L3.
